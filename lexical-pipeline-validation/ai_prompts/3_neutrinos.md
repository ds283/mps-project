Write a technical summary of the detection of electron antineutrinos from a nuclear reactor using a liquid scintillator
detector, for a third-year undergraduate physics student. Use the Chooz experiment as the primary example. Cover the
following topics in order:

Reactor antineutrinos: explain how electron antineutrinos are produced in a nuclear reactor via beta decay of fission
products, and give a rough estimate of the antineutrino flux and energy spectrum.
Neutrino oscillations: summarise the phenomenon of neutrino oscillations using wavefunction mixing. Define the mixing
angle \theta_{13} and explain why a reactor experiment at a baseline of ∼1 km \sim 1 is sensitive to this parameter.
Inverse beta decay: describe the detection reaction \bar{\nu}_e + p \to e^+ + n
and explain how the coincidence between the prompt positron signal and the delayed neutron capture provides
a powerful tool for background rejection.
The scintillator detector: describe the role of the liquid scintillator as both target and detection medium. Explain how
scintillation light is collected by photomultiplier tubes, and discuss why the detector must be heavily shielded and
located underground to reduce backgrounds.
Results and significance: summarise the Chooz result --- a null oscillation signal --- and explain what upper limit this
placed on \theta_{13} at the time. Briefly describe how later experiments such as Double Chooz and Daya Bay improved on
this measurement.
